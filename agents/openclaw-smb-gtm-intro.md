## What Is OpenClaw?

OpenClaw is a personal AI assistant that lives on your computer and connects to the tools you already use — email, calendar, messages, the web. It's not a chatbot you visit on a website. It's more like having a tireless, highly capable team member who is always available, never forgets, and gets smarter the more it learns about your business.

Think of it as the difference between using a calculator and having an accountant.

**What makes it different:**
- It remembers context across conversations — you don't re-explain yourself every time
- It can take action, not just talk — it can draft and send emails, do research, track prospects, pull reports
- It works through channels you already use — Discord, Slack, iMessage, or a web UI
- It's private — running on your own hardware, your data doesn't go to a third party

## Why This Matters for a Small Sales Team

When you're at the stage where the product is proven and the path to meaningful ARR is clear, the bottleneck usually isn't the product — it's sales bandwidth and system. A small team can close deals, but they need leads, follow-up discipline, and market intelligence moving faster than two or three people can manually produce.

OpenClaw fills that gap.

## What OpenClaw Can Do for Your Team

### For Founders / Owners — Business Overview & Strategy

- **Weekly business brief:** A summary of where revenue stands, what's moving, what's stalled — pulled from whatever tracking you use (spreadsheet, CRM, email).
- **Competitive monitoring:** Track what competitors are doing — price changes, new products, promotions — and surface what's relevant to your positioning.
- **Market signals:** Flag relevant industry news, buyer trends, or event opportunities in your target market.
- **Draft communications:** Partner proposals, investor updates, vendor negotiations — first draft in 30 seconds, refine from there.

### For Sales Reps — Acceleration

**Lead generation:**
- Research target accounts before cold outreach — pull info on specific companies and contacts
- Build and maintain a prioritized prospect list, scored by potential deal size
- Find the right contact person at each account (name, LinkedIn, email when available)

**Outreach support:**
- Draft personalized cold emails and follow-ups for each prospect — tailored, not generic
- Track who's been contacted, when, and what happened
- Set follow-up reminders so no lead goes cold

**After the meeting:**
- Summarize call notes into a clean format
- Log the outcome and next action
- Draft the follow-up email while the conversation is still fresh

**Deal intelligence:**
- Instant account brief before a call — "what do I know about this company?"
- Surface news about the prospect that makes your pitch more relevant

**Quota visibility:**
- Daily summary: where you stand vs. the monthly target and what's in the pipeline

## Getting Started — Practical Sequence

You don't need to set up everything at once. Here's what gets value fast:

### Week 1 — Foundation
1. **Install OpenClaw** on one machine. Budget about 20 minutes for initial setup.
2. **Connect email** — this is where most sales activity already lives. OpenClaw reads your inbox and drafts replies.
3. **Give it context** — share your GTM strategy, pricing, and ideal customer profile. It uses this for everything it does.

### Week 2 — Sales Workflow
4. **Build the prospect list** — tell OpenClaw your target categories and geography. Let it research and return a starter list of 30–50 accounts.
5. **Draft outreach templates** — OpenClaw writes the first version of your cold email sequences. Reps review and personalize; OpenClaw handles the drafting.
6. **Set up follow-up tracking** — a simple spreadsheet works fine to start. Have OpenClaw update it.

### Week 3 — Rhythm
7. **Daily standup brief** — each morning, reps get a 3-line summary: who to follow up with today, new leads to add, where they stand vs. monthly target.
8. **Weekly summary** — owner gets a one-page view of MRR progress, accounts added, pipeline health.

### Month 2 — Content & Channels (if applicable)
9. **Content calendar** — builds and maintains a weekly social content plan. Drafts captions, suggests angles, tracks what's performing.
10. **Influencer/partner research** — identify and prioritize relevant voices in your space by audience size, engagement, and fit.

## FAQ

**"Do we need to be technical to use it?"**  
No. You talk to it like a person — in plain English, via chat. If you can text, you can use OpenClaw.

**"Is our sales data private?"**  
Yes. OpenClaw runs on your own machine. Your data doesn't go to any third-party server.

**"What does it cost?"**  
Usage costs are similar to a phone data plan — small if you're asking a few questions a day, larger if running it constantly. Significantly less than hiring a person.

**"How long until we see value?"**  
Week 1. The first time it researches a prospect list or drafts a sales email, you'll feel it.
