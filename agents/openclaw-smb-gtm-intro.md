## What Is OpenClaw?

OpenClaw is a personal AI assistant that lives on your computer and connects to the tools you already use — email, calendar, messages, the web. It's not a chatbot you visit on a website. It's more like having a tireless, highly capable team member who is always available, never forgets, and gets smarter the more it learns about your business.

Think of it as the difference between using a calculator and having an accountant.

**What makes it different:**
- It remembers context across conversations — you don't re-explain yourself every time
- It can take action, not just talk — it can draft and send emails, do research, track prospects, pull reports
- It works through channels you already use — Discord, Slack, iMessage, or a web UI
- It's private — running on your own hardware, your data doesn't go to a third party

- Research target accounts before cold outreach — pull info on specific hotels, caterers, event planners, Airbnb host groups in Las Vegas

**Deal intelligence:**

## Getting Started — Practical First Steps

You don't need to set up everything at once. Here's a sequence that gets value fast:

### Week 1 — Foundation
1. **Install OpenClaw** on one machine (Ricardo's or a shared team machine). Jon can walk through this — it takes about 20 minutes.
2. **Connect email** — this is where most of your sales activity already lives. OpenClaw reads your inbox and can draft replies.
3. **Give it the GTM plan** — share the strategy doc so it knows your targets, pricing, and goals. It will use this as context for everything it does.

### Week 2 — Sales Workflow
4. **Build the B2B prospect list** — tell OpenClaw your target categories (hotels, caterers, Airbnb hosts, event planners) and let it research and build a starter list of 50 accounts in Las Vegas.
5. **Draft the outreach templates** — have OpenClaw write the first version of your cold email sequences. Aaron and Jake review and personalize; OpenClaw handles the drafting.
6. **Set up follow-up tracking** — decide where you want to track prospects (a simple spreadsheet works fine to start) and have OpenClaw update it.

### Week 3 — Rhythm
7. **Daily standup brief** — each morning, Aaron and Jake get a 3-line summary: who to follow up with today, any new leads to add, where they stand vs. the monthly B2B account target.
8. **Weekly business summary** — Ricardo gets a one-page view of MRR progress, B2B accounts added, pipeline health.

### Month 2 — Content & Social
9. **Content calendar** — OpenClaw builds and maintains a weekly TikTok/Reel content plan tied to the "Transform" and "Hack" content pillars. Drafts captions, suggests filming prompts, tracks what performed.
10. **Influencer research** — identify and prioritize Las Vegas food influencers by follower count, engagement rate, and relevance to Vida Meat's audience.
